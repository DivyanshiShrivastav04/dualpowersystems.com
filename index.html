Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about solar energy and battery backup systems

How do solar panels and batteries work together?

Solar panels generate electricity during the day, which is used to power your home and charge the battery system. Excess energy is stored in batteries for use during evening hours or power outages. This creates a self-sufficient energy ecosystem that reduces reliance on the grid.

What kind of battery backup do I need?

The size of your battery backup depends on your home's energy consumption, critical loads you want to power during outages, and your budget. Our experts conduct a detailed energy audit to recommend the optimal battery capacity, typically ranging from 10kWh to 40kWh for residential applications.

Can I go completely off-grid?

Yes, with properly sized solar panels and battery storage, you can achieve complete energy independence. However, most customers choose grid-tied systems with battery backup for reliability and cost-effectiveness. Our team can design both options based on your specific needs and location.

How much does a solar + battery system cost?

System costs vary based on size, equipment quality, and installation complexity. Residential systems typically range from $20,000 to $50,000 before incentives. With federal tax credits and local rebates, net costs can be reduced by 30-50%. Most customers see complete payback within 6-8 years.

What incentives are available?

Current incentives include the 30% federal solar tax credit, state rebates, local utility incentives, and net metering programs. Some areas offer additional incentives for battery storage. Our team helps you navigate all available programs to maximize your savings and return on investment.
